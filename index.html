A Lattice Boltzmann simulation of the air particles over an F1 car during slipstream

1. Macroscopic Properties

From the particle directional distribution \( f_i \), density and momentum are retrieved:

\[ \rho = \sum_{i=0}^{18} f_i \quad \text{and} \quad \mathbf{u} = \frac{1}{\rho} \sum_{i=0}^{18} f_i \mathbf{e}_i \]

2. Equilibrium Distribution (D3Q19)

The target kinetic equilibrium towards which the system relaxes:

\[ f_i^{eq} = w_i \rho \left( 1 + \frac{3 \mathbf{e}_i \cdot \mathbf{u}}{c_s^2} + \frac{9 (\mathbf{e}_i \cdot \mathbf{u})^2}{2 c_s^4} - \frac{3 \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}}{2 c_s^2} \right) \]

3. BGK Collision Operator

Particles collide and shift based on the single relaxation time \( \tau \):

\[ f_i^*(\mathbf{x}, t) = f_i(\mathbf{x}, t) - \frac{1}{\tau} \left( f_i(\mathbf{x}, t) - f_i^{eq}(\mathbf{x}, t) \right) \]

4. Streaming Step

The particle movement function which occurs when no collisions happen:

\[ f_i(\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{e}_i \Delta t, t + \Delta t) = f_i^*(\mathbf{x}, t) \]

5. Momentum Exchange

Forces are measured by integrating particle positions on collisions:

\[ \mathbf{F} = \frac{\delta x}{\delta t} \sum_{\mathbf{x}_b} \sum_{i} \mathbf{e}_i \left( f_i(\mathbf{x}_b) + f_{\bar{i}}(\mathbf{x}_b) \right) \]

6. Quaternion Kinematics

Used to manipulate rotations of the f1 car:

\[ \mathbf{v}' = \mathbf{q} \mathbf{v} \mathbf{q}^{-1} \]

The principles behind slipstream

Slipstream occurs due 2 main steps. One being the pressure difference between the front and back of the F1 vehicle producing drag causing the dirty air which impacts slipstream.

The dirty air of the trailing vehicle creates a region of even lower pressure, which reduces the aerodynamic resistance, allowing for optimal conditions to gain speed against the lead car
